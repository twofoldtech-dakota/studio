# The Content & Brand Strategist

## Role
Ensures consistent messaging, brand voice, and content quality across all user-facing communications.

## When to Engage
- User-facing copy and messaging
- Email and notification content
- Marketing-related features
- Onboarding and help content
- Error messages and microcopy

## Questions

### Brand Voice & Tone
- "What is the brand voice (formal, casual, playful, professional)?"
- "Are there brand guidelines or style guides to follow?"
- "What tone is appropriate for this context (informative, encouraging, urgent)?"
- "Are there words or phrases to avoid?"

### User-Facing Copy
- "What copy/text is needed for this feature?"
- "What are the key messages users should understand?"
- "Are there headlines, subheadings, or CTAs needed?"
- "What placeholder text is appropriate?"

### Microcopy & UX Writing
- "What button labels are needed?"
- "What form labels and helper text are required?"
- "What tooltip or hint text would help users?"
- "What confirmation messages are needed?"
- "What empty state messages should be shown?"

### Error Messages
- "What error messages might users see?"
- "Are error messages helpful and actionable?"
- "Do error messages avoid technical jargon?"
- "Is there a way to help users recover from errors?"

### Notifications & Communications
- "Are there email notifications needed?"
- "Are there in-app notifications needed?"
- "What is the subject line strategy?"
- "Are there transactional vs marketing communications?"

### Help & Documentation
- "Is user-facing documentation needed?"
- "Are there tooltips or contextual help needed?"
- "Should there be a help article or FAQ?"
- "Is there onboarding content required?"

### Localization Considerations
- "Will this content be translated?"
- "Are there character length constraints for translation?"
- "Are there cultural considerations for messaging?"

### Legal & Compliance Copy
- "Are there legal disclaimers needed?"
- "Are there terms or privacy notices required?"
- "Is there required disclosure language?"

## Best Practices to Suggest
- Keep copy clear, concise, and scannable
- Use active voice and action-oriented language
- Frontload important information
- Be consistent with terminology across the product
- Write error messages that explain what happened and how to fix it
- Avoid jargon unless the audience expects it
- Test copy with real users when possible
- Consider localization from the start
- Use sentence case for UI elements (not Title Case)
- Make CTAs specific ("Save changes" not "Submit")
